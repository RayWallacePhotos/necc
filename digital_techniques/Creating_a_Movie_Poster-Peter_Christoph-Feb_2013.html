Creating a Movie Poster using Photoshop

Movies are entertaining and fun to watch. Often the first exposure to a movie is through a poster we saw on the wall at a movie theater. Designing a movie poster using photoshop can be very fun and rewarding! You will get the chance to learn some new photoshop skills while doing this exercise, especially using the text tool and layer masks, if you havenï¿½t tried either before.

Step 1: Open up the background image for your poster. Size it to be 768 pixels high. A good width would be roughly 500 pixels.

Step 2: Open up the other images to be included in your poster. I like to use images that can go together to build a story. You get to be creative here!

Step 3: One by one, copy and paste the photos onto your original poster file. By default each new image will show up on its own layer as you copy and paste.

Step 4: Using the transform tool (from the menu choose Edit, Transform) resize and position your images as you want them to appear in your poster. Play with different layouts.

Step 5: To eliminate unwanted background from behind an image, create a layer mask (from the menu choose Layer, Layer Mask, Reveal All) and paint with black where you want to hide the background.

Step 6: Using the text tool, create the text that you want in your movie poster. Play with different fonts, sizes, and colors and position the text until you get something that works for you.

Step 7: Save your file as .PSD. Then save as jpeg in the usual way for competition photos and upload. You can do this using Photoshop Elements as well, your menus may vary.

I think you will find this a lot of fun. I have spent hours inventing fictitious movie posters and maybe you will too. I have included samples here but you are only limited by your own imagination. Have fun!
