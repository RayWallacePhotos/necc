for the month of April the digital technique is learning how to apply the radial blur filter to create the illusion of movement in an otherwise static photo.
If you were at last night's meeting you saw this demonstrated.
It is a real simple filter but I find it often yields dramatic results.
Here it is:

Open your photo and size it to competition size.

Duplicate the layer by choosing "Duplicate" from the Layers menu.

Make sure the duplicate layer is selected and then go to the Filter menu and choose Blur...Radial.

In the dialog box which pops up, choose:

Amount = 17 (I have found this to be a good starting point, but use whatever number you like...)

Blur method = Zoom

Quality = Best

Apply the filter twice in a row if you like.

Inside the Blur Center window, you must click and drag to reposition the focal point, or "blur center" to your liking.
Hit OK. Observe the results. If you missed with your focal point, then UNDO, and try again.
On the blurred layer you can erase the part of the image which you are trying to keep really sharp, allowing the original sharp image from the layer below to show through.
I have attached dog Naila with and without radial blur applied and you can see the radial blur adds more impact to the running dog.
Play around and have fun!
